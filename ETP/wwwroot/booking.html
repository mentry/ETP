Important cruise information

  • This cruise line requires that passengers younger than 21 years old be accompanied by an adult 21 years or older in the same cabin. All passengers in your cabin must be 6 months or older. Please read the age restrictions for complete details.

  • Cruise lines generally cannot accept guests who will have entered their 24th week of pregnancy by the beginning of or at any time during the cruise. You may be required to product a physician's "Fit to Travel" note prior to sailing. Please read the pregnancy restrictions for complete details.

Traveling with Minors: In the Caribbean, Costa requires that passengers under 21 years old must be accompanied by a parent or guardian in the same cabin unless the passengers under 21 are married and traveling with their spouse or if they are traveling with a parent or guardian in an accompanying cabin. Guests 21 to 24 years old may be required to be accompanied by an adult 25 or older in the same cabin. For non-Caribbean departures, guests under the age of 18 must be accompanied by a parent or guardian in the same cabin, regardless of whether they are married or not.

Minimum Age to Sail: Infants must be at least 6 months of age at the time of embarkation to sail. Infants must be at least 12 months of age at the time of embarkation to sail on the following: trans-ocean crossings (Atlantic, Pacific, Indian, Arctic, etc.)

Pregnancy Policy: Pregnant women are allowed to sail only if pregnant for 24 completed weeks or less on the date of cruise disembarkation. All pregnant women must provide a physician’s letter to Costa Cruise Line at embarkation stating the estimated date of delivery and that the mother and baby are in good health and fit to travel and the pregnancy is not at risk.
